• Twister

Haworthiopsis attenuata
Hawthornia is a small, succulent plant that belongs to the Asphodelaceae family. It is a hybrid between two similar plants, Haworthia and Gasteria, and is native to South Africa. The plant typically grows up to about 6 inches tall and 8 inches wide.

How to care for Twister:

Sunlight

Twister will flourish when exposed to bright, indirect or direct light.

Water

In bright light conditions, water Twister every 2-3 weeks, and wait for the soil to dry out between waterings. Be prepared to water more frequently if the light is intense, and less often if it is less bright.

Humidity

There's no need to worry about it. Normal household humidity is acceptable, and Twister can endure low humidity.

Temperature

If the temperature in your home is between 65°F-75°F and you feel comfortable, then Twister will also feel comfortable.

Tip: Twister needs about 500 ml of water

Here are some typical issues you might face:

Over watering

If the leaves are turning yellow and the potting mix is wet, it might be experiencing root rot due to overwatering.

Under watering

If you notice your plant's leaves are curling and the potting mix is dry, it might be thirsty and not getting enough water.

Soil compaction

Brown or red tips on the leaves can be caused by either underwatering or soil compaction.
